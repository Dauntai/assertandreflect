About

I've worked in software quality for over a decade, spanning multiple stages of the SDLC. This site reflects what I've learned: testing creates confidence, not just defect counts.

I advocate for testers and for systemic quality thinking. Quality is often treated as a checkbox or an afterthought; testers are undervalued. I aim to shift that conversation through practical guidance, strategic frameworks, and reflective essays.

Focus

  • Confidence-driven testing — Testing as a means to enable release decisions, not just find bugs.
  • Risk-aware decision making — Prioritizing what matters and communicating uncertainty clearly.
  • Sustainable automation — Automation as a tool, not an end in itself; design for maintainability.
  • Systemic quality thinking — Quality as a shared responsibility across the organization.
